Our Goal

A strong, diverse Lake Ontario fishery, and a keystone species back where it belongs.

Atlantic Salmon are a native keystone species of deep ecological and cultural importance, including to Indigenous communities around Lake Ontario who have long-standing relationships with this fish. Bringing them back means a healthier lake, healthier streams, and a thriving recreational fishery in both nearshore and offshore waters.

Why This Matters

More fish, more places to catch them, a healthier lake.

Atlantic Salmon are returning in numbers we haven't seen in over a century. Focused stocking of advanced life-stage yearlings, work to increase adult abundance, and continued investment in habitat are building a fishery that delivers incredible shoreline opportunities for anglers alongside a productive offshore boat fishery, all while restoring a species that belongs here.

A Lake-Wide Approach

Working across the lake

Atlantic Salmon don't recognize borders, and neither does the restoration effort. Partners across Lake Ontario work together to improve cold-water habitat, stock fish where they will thrive, and coordinate science from shore to shore.

Together we align on where to stock, what to measure, and how to address the challenges that make natural reproduction difficult. Shared science leads to stronger results and stronger fishing for every angler on the lake.

Adult Atlantic Salmon are returning in growing numbers, contributing to a strong, diverse fishery in both offshore and nearshore waters and creating incredible shoreline opportunities for anglers. More than 300 adults have been captured and sampled over the past three years, giving biologists the best look at this population in a generation.
